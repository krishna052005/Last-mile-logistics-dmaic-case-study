# Last-Mile Logistics Delivery Optimization (Six Sigma DMAIC Case Study)

## Overview
This project presents a case study focused on solving operational inefficiencies in last-mile logistics delivery. The objective was to identify and reduce delivery delays that were causing financial losses and poor customer experience.

The solution applies the **Six Sigma DMAIC framework** to analyze logistics operations and propose data-driven improvements.

---

## Problem Statement
Delivery delays in last-mile logistics were leading to:

- Customer dissatisfaction
- Increased operational costs
- Inefficient delivery routes
- Unpredictable delivery timelines

The challenge was to analyze the delivery process and identify the root causes of delays.

---

## Methodology: Six Sigma DMAIC

### 1. Define
Identified the primary operational issue: **frequent delivery delays in last-mile logistics**.

### 2. Measure
Collected operational insights and delivery performance indicators such as:

- Delivery success rate
- Delay frequency
- Route efficiency
- Workforce availability

### 3. Analyze
Key bottlenecks discovered:

- Address traceability issues
- Workforce absenteeism
- Lack of structured training
- Technology gaps in delivery tracking

### 4. Improve
Proposed improvements included:

- Route optimization strategies
- Improved address validation systems
- Workforce scheduling improvements
- Delivery tracking technology enhancements

### 5. Control
Recommended monitoring mechanisms:

- Delivery performance dashboards
- KPI tracking
- Continuous process evaluation
